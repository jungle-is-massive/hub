Overview

The Advertising Association.

The Advertising Association has represented the UK advertising and marketing industry since 1926. Its council spans the major trade bodies — IPA, ISBA, IAB — alongside broadcasters and tech platforms. It functions as the industry's collective voice to Government, produces research through its think tank Credos, and leads on long-term initiatives around responsibility, trust, and growth.

Two membership products are relevant to Jungle. Front Foot is the AA's senior leader network for UK market access and industry positioning. UKAEG (UK Advertising Export Group) is the international arm — structured missions to global events with curated brand and partner introductions.

They can be taken individually or as a bundle at a reduced combined rate.

What this isn't.

The AA is not an intermediary. Joining does not put Jungle in pitch consideration sets, generate direct briefs, or replicate what Ingenuity+, AAR, or Oystercatchers do. That distinction matters for how we evaluate it.

This is relationship infrastructure — structured access to senior brand leaders, agency peers, and Government contacts we don't currently reach through existing channels. The value compounds over time and runs parallel to intermediary engagement, not instead of it.

The question is whether the access it provides — at key industry moments, at the right seniority level — is worth £4–8k per year at this stage of Jungle's growth.

What it does do: puts Jungle in rooms with senior brand and agency leaders at curated events, gives us a platform to show up as a thought leader rather than a vendor, and provides supporting credibility with intermediaries who already know these networks well.

Option one · UK focus

Front Foot.

The AA's senior leader membership network. Over 100 member businesses spanning advertisers, agencies, media owners, and tech platforms — AMV BBDO, Ogilvy, Dentsu, Meta, TikTok, Sky Media, The Guardian, and more.

Led by Sarah Virani (Head of Membership). On joining, she and the AA's Commercial Director run an in-person induction with Jungle's SLT to map the right events and opportunities against our priorities.

Option two · International focus

UKAEG.

The UK Advertising Export Group. Designed for UK agencies looking to grow internationally — structured missions to major global events with curated brand and partner introductions, market briefings, and collective UK presence.

Led by Aisling Conlon (International Director). In 2025, members reported £40M in new business opportunities generated across the programme, with 30+ senior connections per mission.

The programme runs three core pillars: Inspire (market intelligence), Grow (event and market access), and Market (global visibility and PR).

Value for Jungle

What it unlocks.

Translating the membership benefits into what they practically mean for an agency at Jungle's stage.

Room access
Senior brand and agency leaders at curated events and receptions we'd otherwise have no route into. The kind of relationships that take years to build cold.
Speaking platform
Speaker bootcamps and submission support for Cannes, SXSW, and Athar. Practical help getting Jungle voices onto panels that matter for profile and positioning.
Credibility signal
AA membership carries weight with intermediaries — a shorthand for seriousness within the industry. Useful supporting context as we build those relationships in parallel.
International access
Structured briefings and brand introductions across Cannes, NY, Saudi Arabia, and Austin. Relevant if Jungle has international growth ambitions on the horizon.
Policy intelligence
Early sight of regulatory direction on AI, LHF, data. Useful context for client conversations and for grounding Jungle's own thought leadership in real industry debate.
Industry data
Credos reports, AA/WARC adspend forecasts, and the Trust Tracker — data that can inform our own content and thought leadership outputs.
Export Report
Inclusion in the UK Advertising Export Report — distributed to Government and international partners. Visibility in the right hands.
